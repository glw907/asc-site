The Board of Directors manages the affairs of the corporation. Between 4 and 7 directors serve at any time, with terms staggered to ensure continuity. Elections are held at the annual membership meeting each fall.

The board establishes committees to handle specific areas of club operations. Two standing committees have formal authority defined in the bylaws. The rest are established committees: they run the programs, facilities, and activities that make the club work.

## Officers

The bylaws define four officer positions. The board elects officers annually from among the directors.

**Commodore (Nancy Black).** Principal officer of the corporation. Presides over board and membership meetings and signs contracts on behalf of the club.

**Vice Commodore (Dave Johnson).** Assumes Commodore duties when needed and carries out other duties assigned by the Commodore or board.

**Secretary (Angie Oberlitner).** Issues meeting notices, records official minutes, maintains membership records, and handles club correspondence.

**Treasurer (Matt Flickinger).** Financial officer of the corporation. Manages club funds, oversees accounting, and reports on financial condition at each board meeting.

The board may appoint additional officers and designate their duties.

## Meetings

The board meets quarterly, with additional special meetings as needed. You're welcome to attend and may address the board during designated times.

The annual membership meeting is held each fall. It's the occasion for director elections, financial reporting, and any other business requiring a membership vote.

## Standing Committees

The bylaws establish two standing committees with formal governance authority.

**Finance Committee** (chaired by Matt Flickinger, Treasurer). Oversees the financial health of the corporation. Chaired by the Treasurer per bylaws, with at least two additional members including at least one with accounting expertise. Reports on financial condition at each regular board meeting and periodically assesses internal controls.

**Board Development Committee** (chaired by Geoff Wright). Ensures the health and effectiveness of the board. Consults the board on organizational needs and skills gaps, then independently determines and recommends a candidate slate for director elections. Also oversees board training and periodically reviews the bylaws. The BDC's independence in assembling the candidate slate follows 501(c)(3) best practice.

## Established Committees

These committees have operated continuously for many years and form the core of how the club delivers its programs and maintains its facilities. They're expected to continue from season to season, though the board may adjust their scope or create new committees as club needs evolve in a given year.

To reach a committee chair, look them up in the [member directory](/directory/) or post in the relevant Discord channel.

**Program Committee** (chaired by Christopher Cryan). Plans and instructs sailing courses, coordinates public sailing events, and manages racing. This includes setting the race schedule, issuing sailing instructions, scoring races, and managing race awards.

**Membership Committee** (chaired by Jonathan Ramirez, with Emily Ramirez as co-chair). Reviews membership applications, maintains member information, and assists with new member orientation. Also coordinates social programs including regatta potlucks, Summer Sailstice, the annual membership meeting, and other club gatherings.

**Site Committee** (chaired by Geoff Wright). Creates and executes property development plans, oversees maintenance of club grounds and facilities, and coordinates work parties.

**Harbor Committee** (chaired by TL Stanbro). Maintains and improves docks and moorings. Builds and installs dock sections each spring, removes them each fall, and keeps the hoist operational.

**Fleet Committee** (chaired by Steve Ryan). Maintains the club fleet, manages boat maintenance schedules, and oversees the qualification process. See [Club Boat Use & Qualification](/club-boat-use-and-qualification/) for details.

## Budget Process

Each fall, committees propose budgets as part of the club's annual planning cycle. The board drafts an annual budget from those proposals and circulates the draft to members for comment before adoption. The board then adopts a revised budget based on that feedback. During the season, the board may make adjustments if circumstances warrant. Members receive detailed budget documentation separately.

## Elections

Directors serve staggered three-year terms, so a portion of seats comes up for election each fall. The Board Development Committee manages the process: consulting the board on needs and skills gaps, independently determining the candidate slate, and distributing it to members at least 21 days before voting opens. Electronic voting runs in the week before the annual meeting. Results are announced at the meeting.

Members interested in serving on the board should contact the BDC. That's the entry point for prospective candidates.
