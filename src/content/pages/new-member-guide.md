By the time you finish reading, you should feel ready to plan your first visit with confidence. Once you've been out a few times, the [Visiting the Club](/visiting-the-club/) page is a shorter quick-reference version of the practical details covered here.

## Already Took the Intro Class?

If you learned to sail through one of our beginner courses, your instructors covered most of what's in this guide: boat checkout procedures, safety policies, club customs, and how to get involved. You're already qualified to use the boats you sailed in class.

**This guide is here as a reference** for anything you want to revisit. Feel free to skim the sections below or jump straight to [What's Next](#whats-next) for ideas on your next steps as a member.

## Haven't Taken a Class Yet?

Welcome. You don't need to take a class to be a valued member of the ASC. Plenty of members join for the boats, the camping, the racing, or the community.

Since you didn't have instructors walking you through club operations, **we recommend reading through each section below**, especially the parts about club boats (you'll need a qualification checkout before you can use them) and the volunteer culture (it's how the club runs).

**A resource just for you: the `#education` channel on Discord.** Our instructors actively monitor this channel and are happy to answer questions about sailing, rigging, boat handling, or anything else you're curious about. You don't need to be enrolled in a class to ask. It's the fastest way to get answers from experienced sailors who know our boats and our lake.

If you decide to take a class, check the [Education](/education/) page soon because sessions often fill within a week of registration opening. Your class credit never expires, but spots do.

## Planning Your First Visit

Members and their guests are welcome any time during the sailing season (May through September). No reservations needed. If you're new, we strongly recommend timing your first visit to an [event weekend](/events/). You'll have experienced members around to answer questions, help you rig and launch boats, and generally make your first day easier.

### What to bring

The ASC sits on beautiful lakeside property in Big Lake, about an hour north of Anchorage. It's a rustic site, so come prepared.

- **Drinking water.** No potable water on site, so bring your own. A few gallon jugs or a water cooler will cover most visits. There are a few emergency gallons in the clubhouse; use them if you need to, but please help refill them. Lakeside Laundromat (about ten minutes away, across from Three Bears) has a sink for refilling coolers.
- **Layers and sun protection.** Big Lake weather can shift quickly: warm sun to cold wind in the span of an hour. Bring layers you can add or shed, including a windproof outer layer. Sunscreen matters even on overcast days, because sunlight reflects strongly off the water.
- **Food and snacks.** The clubhouse has a shared fridge for day use: volunteers empty it each weekend, so don't leave food behind. Bring a cooler if you need more space or are staying overnight. Three Bears grocery store is about ten minutes away.
- **Trash bags.** Pack out what you pack in. No dumpster service at the camping area. Use caution with garbage to avoid attracting bears.

### Staying overnight

You're always welcome to stay overnight. We have a lovely camping area with plenty of tent space at the end of Trailer Row. No reservation, no signup. Just show up.

If you're bringing an RV, we have space for 3-4 RVs on a first-come, first-served basis for up to four days. There are specific rules about RV parking. Review the [Transient RV Parking](/visiting-the-club/#transient-rv-parking) section of Visiting the Club before heading out.

## Life at the Club

### Safety first

We're relaxed about most things at the ASC, but safety isn't one of them. A few non-negotiable rules:

All children 12 and under **must** wear a life jacket at all times on club grounds. No exceptions. Our insurance requires it, and more importantly, it's a matter of safety. We will ask families who can't observe this rule to leave the grounds.

All members and guests must wear life jackets on club boats. Adult non-swimmers must wear a life jacket near the dock or water. Drive slowly on club property: kids, dogs, and people are everywhere.

### Bringing guests

Guests are welcome. As the hosting member, you must be on site the entire time your guests are present, and you're responsible for making sure they follow club rules. Guests need to sign a liability form (available in the clubhouse) and confirm they've read the [Member Expectations](/member-expectations/). If you're planning to bring more than five guests at once, contact the board in advance. Guests may ride along on club boats with you, but they cannot sail club boats on their own.

### Dogs at the club

The ASC is an off-leash environment for dogs, and many members bring their canine companions. With that freedom comes responsibility: supervise your dog carefully, clean up all waste (orange bags are provided at the clubhouse and sauna), and confine your dog during potlucks or while you're sailing. Dogs that bark persistently, get into trash, harass other animals or people, or otherwise become a nuisance may need to be removed. Pets are not allowed in the clubhouse. See *Rule XVI: Dogs and Pets* in the [Member Expectations](/member-expectations/) for full details.

### Join the ASC Discord server

The ASC maintains a [Discord server](/discord-server/) for informal discussion of all sailing topics, as well as specific club-related discussions around classes, clinics, racing, club boats, and site maintenance. You should have received an invite when you signed up for membership. If you haven't taken a sailing class yet, the `#education` channel is a great place to start: instructors and experienced sailors are there to help with any questions.

### Respect your fellow sailors

We welcome all current or aspiring sailors regardless of the color of their skin, their gender, their country of origin, their politics, their religion, or their sexual orientation. We're a remarkably diverse group, united by a passionate love of sailing. You'll fit right in as long as you're respectful, responsible, and safe while at the club.

## Club Boats

Our fleet is available to all qualified members at no extra charge: Lido 14s, Lasers, Optimists, a Buccaneer 18, a Catalina 16.5, a Skipjack 15, and an Ensign 22. To use a boat independently, you need to be qualified on it. Completing our Intro to Sailing class qualifies you on the boats you sailed. If you have prior experience but didn't take a class, post in `#education` on Discord to schedule a checkout.

A few key things to know:

- **Two-hour blocks.** Note your checkout time on the clubhouse whiteboard and check whether anyone is waiting before going longer.
- **Life jackets required** at all times on all club boats. No exceptions.
- **Guests are welcome** on club boats but must sail with a qualified member.
- **Never leave a boat at the dock overnight.** Haul out and put it away when you're done.

If something is missing, damaged, or broken, report it in `#fleet` on Discord or through [Issues & Support](/issues-and-support/?category=boats#report-an-issue).

## The Volunteer Culture

The ASC runs entirely on volunteer power. We don't have any paid staff, so volunteers do all the work at the club: fixing docks, teaching classes, cleaning up after potlucks, maintaining club boats, mowing the grass, and much more.

At minimum, we ask all members to attend at least one of the two annual work parties (Spring and Fall). But we hope you'll also find a small project or two to work on every time you visit. You don't need to ask permission or sign up. If you see something that needs doing, go for it.

Here are some things you can do during a typical visit:

- Split and stack firewood
- Stock wood in the sauna or clubhouse
- Check the grill propane and replace it if it's low (pick up a new tank at Three Bears and submit your receipt on the [Issues & Support](/issues-and-support/?category=reimbursement#report-an-issue) page for reimbursement)
- Cut some grass or trim weeds
- Pull any visible vetch from the shoreline
- Restock clubhouse supplies
- Clean and tidy the clubhouse or sauna
- Clean any of the club boats
- Refill boat or lawnmower gas (submit your receipt on the [Issues & Support](/issues-and-support/?category=reimbursement#report-an-issue) page for reimbursement)
- Help with anything else that looks like it needs doing

Even 20 minutes of work makes a difference. Members who actively contribute through work parties, volunteering, and general helpfulness get priority consideration for storage spots, moorings, and other limited resources. It's not a formal point system. It's just how a volunteer organization works.
