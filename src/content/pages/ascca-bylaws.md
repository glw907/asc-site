[Plain-English summary]
The Alaska Sailing Club Condominiums Owners Association (ASCCA) is a separate legal entity — a condominium association that governs the property on which the club operates. The ASC is a unit owner within this structure, holding Unit H. These bylaws define how the ASCCA is governed and what authority it has over the property.

**What this means for members:**

- **The ASCCA governs the physical property, not club operations.** Day-to-day sailing activities, membership, and programs are governed by the ASC bylaws. The ASCCA handles common area maintenance, shared assessments, and permanent physical modifications.
- **The ASC controls the ASCCA.** Unit H carries seven votes; all other units carry one vote each. This gives the club effective control over most ASCCA decisions.
- **Physical changes to the property** — anything involving common elements or permanent structures — may require ASCCA approval, not just an internal board decision.
- **The ASCCA can levy assessments** on unit owners, including the ASC, for shared property costs such as maintenance and insurance.
- **The ASCCA board** has three members, elected annually by unit owners. As the dominant voter, the ASC effectively determines who sits on the ASCCA board.

## Article I: Introduction

These bylaws govern the Alaska Sailing Club Condominiums Common Interest Community. Capitalized terms used but not defined here have the meanings given in the Declaration's Article I.

## Article II: Executive Board

### Structure

The board consists of three members until Declarant control ends, then remains at three. A majority of directors must be Unit Owners, except for directors appointed by the Declarant. Directors are elected annually by Unit Owners, with at least one-third of terms expiring each year.

### Powers and Duties

The board may adopt rules and regulations, establish budgets, and collect assessments. It may hire managers and staff, institute litigation, make contracts, and regulate Common Elements. The board may also grant easements and licenses, impose fees and fines, provide indemnification, and exercise other powers necessary for governance of the Association.

### Meetings

Directors must meet within ten days after the annual Unit Owner meeting. Special meetings require three business days' notice. All meetings occur in Big Lake unless directors consent otherwise. A majority of directors constitutes a quorum.

## Article III: Unit Owners

### Meetings

The first annual meeting occurs within sixty days after the Declarant conveys seventy-five percent of Phase 1 Lots. Subsequent annual meetings are held at a time set by the board. Special meetings may be called by the president, a majority of the board, or twenty percent of voting members.

### Notice

Meetings require ten to thirty days' written notice, delivered by mail or hand delivery to the address of record for each Unit Owner.

### Voting

Unit H (Class A) carries seven votes. All other units carry one vote per unit. Where a unit has multiple owners, those owners must agree by majority before casting the unit's vote. Proxies are valid for up to one year unless a shorter term is specified.

### Quorum

One-half of all votes entitled to be cast constitutes a quorum for Unit Owner meetings.

## Article IV: Officers

### Positions

The principal officers are president, vice president, secretary, and treasurer. Officers are elected annually by the board. The president and vice president need not be board members, but the president and secretary may not be the same person.

### Responsibilities

**President** — chief executive officer of the Association. Presides over all meetings of the board and Unit Owners.

**Vice President** — assumes the duties of the president when the president is unavailable.

**Secretary** — maintains official records of the Association and executes documents on its behalf.

**Treasurer** — manages Association funds, maintains financial records, and makes required deposits.

## Article V: Enforcement

### Violations

Following notice and a hearing, the board may enter units to abate dangerous conditions or seek injunctive relief for violations of the governing documents.

### Fines

The board may levy reasonable fines for persistent violations of the governing documents after providing notice and an opportunity for a hearing.

## Article VI: Indemnification

Directors and officers are indemnified by the Association in accordance with Alaska Statutes sections AS 10.20.051(b) and AS 10.20.011(14).

## Article VII: Records

### Maintenance

The Association maintains financial records audited as provided in Article XVII of the Declaration. Unit Owners may examine Association records during business hours after providing reasonable notice.

### Required Records

The Association keeps the following records: unit owner accounts; assessment payment history; capital expenditure records; reserve accounts; financial statements; current budgets; judgments against the Association; insurance coverage; alterations that violate the Declaration; code violations; maintenance costs; annual balance sheets (retained for ten years); tax returns; and minutes of board and member meetings.

## Article VIII: Miscellaneous

### Notices

Notices to the Association are delivered to the manager's office or the Association's address of record. Notices to Unit Owners are sent to their recorded addresses. Notices to security interest holders are sent via certified mail.

### Office

The principal office is located on the property or elsewhere as the board designates.

### Non-Waiver

Failure to enforce any provision of these bylaws does not constitute a waiver of the right to enforce it in the future.

## Article IX: Amendments

These bylaws may be amended only in accordance with Article XV of the Declaration.
