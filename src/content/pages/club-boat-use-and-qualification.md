title: "Club Boat Use and Qualification"
---

Our fleet is available to all qualified members at no extra charge. We have Lido 14s, Lasers, Optimists, a Buccaneer 18, a Catalina 16.5, a Skipjack 15, and an Ensign 22.

## Getting Qualified

**Completed an Intro to Sailing class?** You're already checked out on the boats you sailed. No further steps needed for those boats.

**Have prior sailing experience?** Post in `#education` on Discord to schedule a checkout with an instructor. For each boat type, you'll need to demonstrate:

- Rigging and launching (including hoist use for the Lidos, Buccaneer 18, Skipjack, and Catalina 16.5)
- Capsize recovery
- Upwind and downwind sailing
- Safe docking
- Proper storage and put-away

## Rules

- **Two-hour blocks.** Note your checkout time on the clubhouse whiteboard. Check whether anyone is waiting before going longer.
- **Life jackets required** at all times on all club boats. No exceptions. Repeated violations result in loss of membership.
- **Guests are welcome** on club boats but must sail with a qualified member. Guests may not take boats out on their own.
- **Lasers and Optimists** may not be left unattended on the small boat launch dock. They may be left in front of the small boat rack with sails down.
- **Lidos and the Catalina** may be left at the dock during the day with boats properly secured and sails down.
- **No boats overnight at the dock.** Lidos and the Catalina must be hauled out and put away at the end of the day, even if you plan to use them again the next morning.

## Docking and Put-Away

::::steps
:::step[At the dock]
Secure boats with a bow line, a stern line, and a spring line. Attach a fender where the boat might touch the dock. If you're unsure how, ask any experienced member.

If you're leaving a Laser unattended, even briefly, release the clew and outhaul fully so the sail flies free of the boom. This prevents wind gusts from damaging the boat.
:::
:::step[When you're done]
Never leave a club boat at the dock overnight.

- Haul the boat out and put it away in its designated spot
- Stow sails in the sail shed
- Clean the boat and leave it tidy for the next member
- Check that all equipment is accounted for

If anything is missing or damaged, report it in `#fleet` on Discord or through [Issues & Support](/issues-and-support/?category=boats#report-an-issue).
:::
::::

## Ensign 22

Getting checked out on the Ensign (our club keelboat) is more involved than a dinghy checkout. It's a comfortable boat under most conditions, but it's large, heavy, and complex, and the consequences of mistakes are higher than with a dinghy. We require at least one year of active sailing experience before starting the qualification process. Contact the Education Vice Commodore when you're ready.

## Pontoon Boat and RIB

The pontoon boat and instructional RIB are essential safety tools. We like to have as many qualified operators as possible. Contact the Harbormaster or Education Vice Commodore to get certified. They'll cover motor and pump operation, safety equipment, docking technique, and, for the RIB, hoist and lifting harness use.
