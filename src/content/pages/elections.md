title: "Director Elections"
description: "How the nomination process, electronic voting, and annual results work."
---

ASC is a member-governed nonprofit. The directors you elect set the club's direction, manage the budget, and make decisions on behalf of the membership. Elections happen each fall. Here's how the whole process works.

## What the Board Does

The board runs the club. Directors set priorities, approve the budget, manage club property, and make policy decisions throughout the year. Between 4 and 7 directors serve at any given time.

Terms are three years. Seats are staggered so only a portion of the board comes up for election each fall, which keeps institutional knowledge on the board from year to year instead of replacing everyone at once. No director may serve more than two consecutive full terms.

Officers, Commodore, Vice Commodore, Secretary, and Treasurer, are chosen by the board from among the directors each year. You're not separately electing a Commodore; you're electing directors, and the board then decides who fills each officer role.

## How Candidates Are Nominated

The Board Development Committee (BDC) manages the nomination process. The BDC consults the board on what skills and experience are needed, then independently identifies and recommends a candidate slate. The slate reflects the BDC's judgment, not the board's. That's intentional: it keeps sitting directors from picking their own successors.

The slate is published at least 21 days before voting closes, both by email and on the website.

If you're interested in serving on the board, contact the BDC. It's the starting point for prospective candidates.

## How Voting Works

Voting is electronic and open to all voting members. The Secretary opens voting no later than 8 days before the annual meeting; it closes no later than 24 hours before the meeting begins.

For the result to be valid, at least 15% of authorized voting members must cast a ballot. This threshold is called a quorum. If quorum isn't met, the election falls back to a secret ballot at the annual meeting itself.

You vote for as many candidates as there are open seats. The candidates with the highest vote totals win. If more candidates are nominated than seats are available, only enough candidates to fill the vacant positions (up to the 7-director maximum) are elected.

## Results

Results are announced at the annual meeting and recorded in the minutes. Newly elected directors take their seats immediately upon announcement.

## Timeline

Working backward from the annual meeting, held each fall:

| Milestone | Timing |
|---|---|
| BDC consults board on organizational needs | Early fall |
| BDC publishes candidate slate | At least 21 days before voting closes |
| Electronic voting opens | No later than 8 days before the annual meeting |
| Electronic voting closes | No later than 24 hours before the annual meeting |
| Results announced | At the annual meeting |
| New directors take their seats | Immediately upon announcement |

The voting window is at least 7 days. Exact dates vary by year depending on when the annual meeting is scheduled.
