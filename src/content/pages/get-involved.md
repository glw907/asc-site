Committees are how we get things done: teaching classes, running races, building docks, and keeping the boats in shape. Participation is the path to real relationships at the club and a genuine say in how things run.

## Committees

Each committee is chaired by a member and open to any member in good standing. Most committees meet seasonally or as projects require, we're not a weekly-meeting kind of organization. The easiest way to join is to reach out to the chair through the [member directory](/directory/) or post a question in the relevant Discord channel.

## How Projects Happen

Most club projects start with a committee. The committee identifies a need, develops a proposal, and brings it to the board with a budget request. The board approves or adjusts, and the committee executes. That's the path from idea to reality at the club. More accessible than it sounds.

**The annual planning cycle** runs each fall before the general membership meeting. Committees assess what worked, what they want to tackle next year, and what it will cost. Each committee submits a project list with estimated budgets. The board reviews the full picture across all committees, balances it against available funds, and sets the annual budget, which is presented to members at the fall meeting before it's finalized.

**Mid-season requests** follow the same logic on a shorter timeline. A committee identifies an unplanned need, drafts a brief proposal with scope and cost, and brings it to the next regular board meeting. The board approves or adjusts. Urgent safety issues can move faster through officer action, ratified at the next meeting.

**What a proposal looks like:** it doesn't need to be formal. A clear statement of the problem, a description of the solution, a rough cost estimate, and who's going to do the work. Committees handle the write-up; the board asks questions and decides. Most proposals pass.

**After approval**, the committee executes: scheduling work parties, ordering materials, coordinating volunteers. Progress is visible in the relevant Discord channels (`#site`, `#fleet`, `#harbor`), so any member can see what's active and jump in if they want to help.

**If you have an idea but aren't on a committee yet,** bring it to the committee whose scope it falls under. Post in the relevant Discord channel or reach out to the chair through the [member directory](/directory/). If the idea is compelling, committees will often carry it through the process themselves.

## Serving on the Board

The Board of Directors sets club policy, approves budgets, manages club affairs, and is ultimately responsible for the organization. Directors serve three-year terms. It's a volunteer role, with quarterly board meetings plus whatever time a specific project or issue requires.

The Board Development Committee manages the election each fall: it consults the board on organizational needs and skills gaps, identifies candidates, and publishes a slate before voting opens. Members interested in serving should contact the BDC. That's the entry point.
