The ASC Discord server is where members ask rigging questions, report dock issues, share photos, and stay connected between visits to Big Lake. It's informal, friendly, and entirely optional: official club updates always go out by email and on the website. You should have received an invitation link with your membership welcome email. If not, request one below.

## Getting Started

::::steps

New to Discord? The [Beginner's Guide](https://support.discord.com/hc/en-us/articles/360045138571-Beginner-s-Guide-to-Discord) covers the basics.

**Nickname convention:** The ASC server uses real names: it helps members connect your messages to the person they know from the club. Please set yours right away: in any channel, type `/nick` and press Enter, then enter your name.

## Channels

Channels are topic-specific conversation rooms listed in the sidebar. Unlike a group text that disappears, they stay open indefinitely and you can scroll back to catch up on anything you missed. When a channel has new messages, it appears bold; once you've read them, it goes quiet. You don't need to follow all of them. The ASC server organizes channels into four groups:

**info** (read-only)

- `#start-here`: Server guidelines for new members. Read this first.

**community** (day-to-day conversation)

- `#general`: Sailing topics, questions, photos, and official club updates.
- `#introductions`: Introduce yourself when you join.
- `#education`: Classes, clinics, and sailing questions.
- `#racing`: Sailboat racing discussion.
- `#buccaneer-18`: The ASC's one-design class.
- `#lost-and-found`: Lost or found items at the club.
- `#off-topic`: Everything else.

**infrastructure** (fleet, grounds, docks, and moorings)

- `#fleet`: Club boats. Report damage or needed repairs here.
- `#site`: Clubhouse, grounds, sauna, and hoist. Report site problems here.
- `#harbor`: Docks, moorings, and ramps. Report harbor issues here.
- `#technology`: IT coordination for members supporting ASC systems.

**video chat** (live channels)

- `#general`: Open voice/video for sailing and club discussion.
- `#meetings`: Reserved for official board and committee meetings.

## Notifications

The ASC Discord is quiet by default: you'll only be notified when someone mentions you directly (types `@YourUsername` in a message to ping you) or when a message goes to `@everyone`.

New posts published to the website are announced automatically in `#general`, or `#racing` for race reports and results, by **Boson Bot**, the club's automated helper. These show up as *embeds*: a boxed card with a title, a short excerpt, and a link to the full post, rather than a plain chat message. Boson Bot sends them with an `@everyone` ping, so you'll receive them without any extra setup.

![Boson Bot, the Discord server automated helper.](media:boson-bot-avatar.80aada3ca9cc0d0a)

If you want notifications beyond `@everyone` messages, say, every post in `#racing` or `#education`, you can opt in per channel or per member.

**To turn up notifications:**

- Channel (mobile): Long-press the channel name → **Notifications** → **All Messages**
- Channel (desktop): Right-click the channel name → **Notification Settings** → **All Messages**
- Member: Right-click their username → **Notification Settings** → **All Messages**

## Server Rules

Our Discord is an extension of the ASC community. By joining, you agree to:

- **Be respectful and welcoming.** Harassment or bullying will not be tolerated.
- **Keep it family-friendly.** Use appropriate language and share suitable content.
- **Stay on topic.** Use `#off-topic` for anything unrelated to sailing or the club.
- **No spam or ads.** Repeated messages and unrelated promotions are not allowed.
- **Respect privacy.** Don't share other members' personal details without permission.
- **Use a clear identity.** A recognizable nickname and appropriate profile picture help everyone know who you are.
- **Follow staff guidance.** Moderators have the final say on disputes.

We also follow the [Discord Community Guidelines](https://discord.com/guidelines).
