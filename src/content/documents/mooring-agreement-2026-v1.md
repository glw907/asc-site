# Alaska Sailing Club—Mooring Agreement

**Season: 2026.** This agreement is between Alaska Sailing Club, Inc. ("the Club")
and the member signing below ("I," "me"). It covers one seasonal mooring assignment
for the boat I identify in my signature record, and I sign it each season with my
mooring fee. In the release and indemnity sections, "the Club" includes its
officers, directors, volunteers, and members.

## 1. The assignment

The Club assigns me one mooring for the 2026 season, administered by the
Harbormaster. The assignment is personal to me: it is not transferable, it does not
carry over to a new owner if I sell my boat, and it requires that I hold Active
Participating Member status and keep the mooring in active use through the season.

## 2. What the Club owns, and what I own

**The Club owns the mooring up to and including the mooring ball.** Everything
beyond the ball is mine: the pendant, lines, shackles, chafe gear, and the boat
itself.

I am solely responsible for the adequacy, condition, and maintenance of my own gear
beyond the ball, and for my boat's suitability for an open swing mooring on Big
Lake. The Club does not inspect, approve, or advise on my gear or my boat.

## 3. No custody, no bailment

I attach and detach my own boat. The Club never takes possession, custody, or
control of it. This agreement is a license to use a mooring space, not a promise to
keep, watch, or care for my boat, and it creates no bailment.

**The mooring field is not a secure or attended facility.** There is no fence, no
attendant, no monitoring, and no one standing watch. My boat and everything aboard
it stay at the mooring at my own risk.

## 4. The risks I accept

**Any part of a mooring system can fail—including the part the Club owns.** Severe
weather and extreme wake happen on Big Lake, and a mooring that has held for years
can still drag or part. The Club makes no representation that any mooring is
adequate for my boat, or for any weather or wake condition.

I accept these risks:

- **Failure of any part of the mooring system, expressly including the club-owned
  ground tackle**, and failure of my own pendant, lines, shackles, or fittings.
- **Break-away**: my boat coming free and drifting, grounding, sinking, or striking
  other boats, moorings, docks, or property.
- **Weather, wake, and ice.** Wind, waves, storms, wake from passing traffic, and
  ice early and late in the season.
- **Contact with adjacent vessels**, and damage caused by another boat breaking
  free.
- **Theft, vandalism, swamping, and sinking.**
- **Cold-water immersion**—cold shock and hypothermia—while rowing out, tending,
  boarding, or leaving my boat.

## 5. My release of the Club, including for its negligence

**I release the Club from all claims for damage to or loss of my boat and property,
and for injury or death arising from my use of the mooring—including claims that
the Club was negligent.** This release is not limited to the risks inherent in
mooring a boat: it expressly covers the Club's own ordinary negligence in selecting,
installing, inspecting, and maintaining the club-owned ground tackle and the
mooring field.

This release does not apply to gross negligence, recklessness, or intentional
misconduct, and it does not waive any right that Alaska law does not allow me to
waive. It binds me, my heirs, my dependents, and my personal representatives, and I
will not sue the Club over anything released here.

## 6. My indemnification of the Club

**I am responsible for harm my boat causes.** If my boat—including
after a break-away—damages another vessel, mooring, dock, or any other property,
or injures any person, and a claim of any kind is brought against the Club because
of it, I will defend and indemnify the Club and pay its costs, including reasonable
attorney's fees. The same applies to claims arising from my own conduct or my
guests' conduct on or around the mooring field.

## 7. Club operations

The Club may move my boat or my mooring when reasonably necessary for club
activities, safety, or compliance with law, and will notify me when it does. I
consent to those moves. A move under this section does not create custody of my
boat or any ongoing duty of care, and this agreement's release applies to it.

## 8. Insurance—board decision, presented both ways

> **OPTION A—insurance required.** I will carry liability insurance covering my
> boat while moored, with limits of at least $[300,000] per occurrence, for the full
> season. I will provide the Club a current certificate of insurance [naming Alaska
> Sailing Club, Inc. as an additional insured / on request] before my boat goes on
> the mooring, and my coverage lapsing ends my mooring assignment.

> **OPTION B—insurance strongly recommended, not required.** Insurance on my boat,
> including liability coverage, is my responsibility. The Club does not insure my
> boat and strongly recommends I carry my own coverage; if my uninsured boat causes
> harm, Section 6 still applies in full.

## 9. Season end, removal, and abandonment

My boat comes off the mooring by the season-end date the Harbormaster publishes. If
it remains after that date, or after my mooring assignment otherwise ends, the Club
will send notice to my contact information on file; if the boat still remains
[30] days after notice, the Club may haul, move, and store it at my risk and
expense, and my boat becomes stored property subject to the lien, deemed-abandonment,
and disposition terms of the Dry Storage Agreement, which are incorporated here.

## 10. Rules and contact information

I will follow the mooring rules set by the Harbormaster and the board. I will keep
my contact information current with the Club, and I agree that notice sent to the
email address and phone number on file is notice to me.

## Signing

governed by Alaska law.
