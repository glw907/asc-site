# Alaska Sailing Club—Trailer Row Use Guidelines

**Season: 2026.** I sign this each season with my long-term RV space, alongside the
Dry Storage Agreement. Signing these guidelines is a condition of holding a Trailer
Row space.

Trailer Row sits on Matanuska-Susitna Borough land that the Club manages under
Management Agreement MSB006789 and the Borough's land use permit conditions. These
rules exist so the Club keeps that agreement; breaking them puts every member's use
of the land at risk.

**I have read these guidelines, and I agree to follow all of them:**

- **Active use.** My RV is used at least six nights per season. Trailer Row is not
  dead storage.
- **Livable condition.** My RV stays in livable condition, with sanitary interior
  and exterior conditions maintained at all times.
- **Clean space.** The area around my RV stays clean and tidy. No trash; I pack out
  what I pack in, and remove trash regularly.
- **No fuel storage.** No portable storage of gasoline, kerosene, or diesel.
  Propane in fit-for-purpose canisters is acceptable. I report any fuel release to
  an ASC board member immediately.
- **Fire safety.** My RV carries a working fire extinguisher at all times.
- **No firearms discharge, explosives, or fireworks** along Trailer Row.
- **No gray water discharge** anywhere on club grounds.
- **Secured when away.** I lock my RV when not in use and put away sliders and
  awnings after each use.
- **Season end.** I disconnect electrical hookups at the end of each season.
- **Always movable.** My RV remains movable on short notice at all times.
- **The 72-hour rule.** If the Mat-Su Borough requires it, my RV will be removed
  within 72 hours of notice. If I fail to comply, it will be towed at my expense.

I understand that my space does not transfer with RV ownership, that a replacement
RV must be inspected before placement, and that holding a space requires Active
Participating Member status, paid dues, and compliance with club bylaws and rules.
