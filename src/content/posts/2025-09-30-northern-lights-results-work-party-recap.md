Wow! What an amazing way to close out the racing season. We couldn’t have asked for better conditions — we had mixed sun and clouds, with 5-10 knots of breeze. (We even had a few zippier gusts that provided some good planing moments.) The whole day significantly outperformed the dismal forecast.

Attendance was also higher than expected, with thirteen teams at the skippers meeting. That tied last year for the best turnout in the history of the ASC’s newest regatta. Our original plan had been to race a single Portsmouth fleet (that’s what the SIs called for), but with the great turnout we decided to race two fleets, with seven boats in Portsmouth and six racing in the Bucc fleet.

The course took the fleet around Saddleback, Burston, and Bird islands. Racing was tight, and the ‘round-the-island format generated lots of interesting decision-points while working with shifts and wind-shadows near the islands. Even the start line presented in interesting decision — the dock end of the line was heavily favored, but the breeze was much stronger and more consistent at the pin end. In the first race, Steve Ryan / Hannah Wright went for the dock and, and the rest of the fleet went hard for the pin. Given that, I’ll let *you* guess which side worked out better. (Hint: Steve and Hannah won the Bucc fleet but a solid margin.)

We managed two full races, with races winners finishing in less than 30 minutes. In Portsmouth, Kara Norby took 1st on a Laser, Raia Stamm took 2nd, also on a Laser, and Ben & Charolette took 3rd in Waldo, the club Bucc. On the Buccaneer 18 class, Steve Rayan and Hannah Wright took 1st, Gabe and Jake Black took 2nd, and Cathy and Geoff Wright took 3rd. (Racing was so tight that we managed a rare perfect tie on the Bucc fleet. In the 1st race, Cathy and I tied with Gabe and Jake for 2nd.)

You can see full race results at the end of this post. Thanks to Joyce Cryan and Paul Willing for serving as race committee, and thanks to Brie Busey for serving as PRO (principal race officer).

## Work Party Highlights

Well, we couldn’t dodge the rain for the whole weekend. Sunday was cool, grey and drizzly. But turnout was strong, and we accomplished most of our planned task including: folding in the docks, cleaning the clubhouse, sauna and grill, pulling the Ensign and pontoon boat, winterizing engines, putting away picnic tables, and flipping the Lidos for winter storage.

In addition to all of the usual fall put-away tasks, we did some gentle grading in the boat parking and hoist area to make it easier to move boats. (And a big thanks Josh and the whole Carter family for bringing out their tractor and box grader to make this possible.) While we had the tractor available, we also did some more aggressive dirt work to set up the old Lido rack space as future boat parking.

### Future work

We have small crew planning to head out in a few weeks to take care of a few small tasks, including:

- *Taking down the picnic awnings.* We didn’t do that during the work party because we didn’t want to pack them wet.

- *Move the mooring brake rotors from the small boat area to the “project materials” area.*

- *Trim the shoreline willows.* (According to our ADF&G instructors, we’re supposed to wait until after the first hard frost to do this to reduce risk of pests or diseases because of the fresh cuts.)

- *Over-seed the graded areas.* Josh did a great job preserving most of the grass, smoothing some of the bigger bumps definitely removed some grass. We’re going to add some seed this fall and again in the spring to fill in these areas.

I *think* that’s it, but if anybody else thinks of anything else, just throw it in the `#site` channel. And thanks to everybody who made it out! (Oh, and thanks to Emily, Jonathan, and Commodore Nancy for grillin’ up work party lunch.)
