Ahoy, sailors! Below are the results for both the Fireweed Ladies Race and the Governor’s Cup. (Apologies for the delay in posting the Fireweed results.) Both regattas were great fun.

For the Fireweed, we had nine Buccaneers on the line and eighteen racers total. That’s the largest one-design turnout we’ve ever had for this event and one of the best overall. Conditions were favorable: the first race was light, but the rest featured a steady and sometimes lively breeze.

The Governor’s Cup was smaller than usual, with many regulars away traveling or at cross-country running meets. Winds were light, but it was still a fine weekend of racing. Highlights included time around the campfire and making progress on site and dock work while waiting for the wind.

Looking ahead, we have a small dock-framing party this Saturday from 11:00 a.m. to 5:00 p.m. The goal is to build frames for two 20-foot dock sections to replace aging plywood docks at the hoist end, plus one extra-long (20-foot) finger dock to accommodate both dinghies and keelboats. Materials and floats are on-site. The new frames will sit on shore for the winter. We'll deck and attach them in the spring.

We’ll need five or six capable carpenters to get it done. Dave Johnson is leading the effort, with coordination happening in the `#harbor` channel [on Discord](/members/discord-server/).
