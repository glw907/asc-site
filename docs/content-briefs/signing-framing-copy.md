# Brief: signing-flow framing copy

Piece: the words inside the member signing moment (welcome, per-document framing
lines, progress, sign row, contact-confirm, completion, portal row). Drafted for the
waivers sitting (T7); the T4 build implements these words verbatim. The attorney
reviews the framing lines beside the documents, because a framing line that
misstates legal effect is worse than none.

## Verifiable facts

- Documents, 2027 season: Release of Liability and Assumption of Risk (every adult;
  a parent additionally signs Part Two per minor child); Club Rules Acknowledgement
  (every member, annually); Mooring Agreement (mooring holders, with the mooring
  fee); Dry Storage Agreement (storage holders, with the storage fee); Trailer Row
  Use Guidelines (RV space holders); Trailered Boat Parking Rules (boat-parking
  holders); Rack Storage Rules (rack holders); Youth Medical & Emergency Form
  (parent, per child, per season, at class signup).
- The flow: full document text shown in the page; the member types their legal name
  and clicks Sign; one signature per document; progress shown as "N of M"; fresh
  signatures each season; class signup presents nothing when the current-season
  release is already on file.
- The record: the club stores the exact text shown, the name as typed, and the date
  and time.
- Contact-confirm (storage and mooring holders only): shown once at the end,
  prefilled from the profile; the reason is the Borough's 72-hour move-out clause—
  the club must be able to reach the member (stated as a covenant in the storage
  agreement).
- Signing happens inside join, renewal, class signup, and asset-fee payment; the
  portal "Needs your attention" row links to one page that clears everything
  outstanding.

## Audience questions

What am I about to do, and how long does it take? What is this document, and why
does the club ask me to sign it? What happens when I click Sign? Why is it asking
about my contact info? Am I done?

## The one next step

Per document: read it and sign it. At the end: back to whatever the member was
doing (payment, signup, the portal).

## Container plan

Portal signing page in the T6b text-action register: welcome header (one or two
sentences), per-document section (title + one framing line + full-text scroll region
+ sign row), progress marker, contact-confirm card (one line + fields + confirm
action), completion line, portal attention row (one line). Budgets: welcome ≤ 2
sentences; framing lines ≤ 2 sentences; all microcopy single-line.

## Hard rule carried from the design

A framing line names what the document is and why the club asks. It never
paraphrases, summarizes, or softens what the document legally does—no "this just
means", no "standard waiver", no reassurance.
