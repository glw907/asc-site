# Alaska Sailing Club—Release of Liability and Assumption of Risk

**Season: 2027.** I sign this document once per season, for myself. If I am a parent
or guardian signing for a minor in my household, Part Two also applies, and I sign it
separately for each child.

In this document, "the Club" means Alaska Sailing Club, Inc. and its officers,
directors, instructors, coaches, committee members, volunteers, and members.

## Part One—My own agreement

### The activities

The Club is a volunteer-run sailing club on Big Lake, Alaska. Its activities include
sailing and boating; sailing instruction and training, including on-water drills and
capsize-recovery practice; use of club boats and equipment; racing and race-committee
work; work parties and site maintenance; and social events, camping, and general use
of the club grounds, docks, and waterfront.

### The risks

**Big Lake is cold, deep, and remote.** These activities can injure or kill me. The
risks include:

- **Cold-water immersion.** Sudden immersion can cause cold shock—involuntary
  gasping, panic, and loss of swimming ability within seconds—and hypothermia
  follows quickly. Big Lake stays cold enough for both all season. Immersion can be
  fatal even for strong swimmers wearing life jackets.
- **Capsize, swamping, falls overboard, and drowning.**
- **Collision** with other boats, docks, moorings, buoys, or submerged objects, and
  injury from booms, rigging, winches, and lines under load.
- **Weather.** Wind, waves, squalls, lightning, and rapid weather changes.
- **Instruction.** Sailing classes practice the real thing, including capsize and
  recovery drills in open water, in cold water, on boats I may be unfamiliar with.
- **Equipment and grounds.** Hoists, ramps, docks, floats, trailers, uneven and
  slippery ground, campfires, and vehicle and trailer movement on club property.
- **Work parties.** Hand and power tools, chainsaws, ladders, heavy lifting, and
  construction and maintenance work performed by volunteers.
- **Other people.** The acts and omissions of other members, students, guests, and
  visitors, on and off the water.
- **Remoteness.** The club site is rural. Emergency responders can take a long time
  to arrive, and rescue on the water may depend on whoever happens to be present.

There is no complete list. I accept these risks and the other risks inherent in
these activities, whether or not they are written here.

### No supervision, no safety promises

The Club has no paid staff. No one inspects my boat or equipment, supervises my
sailing, or stands rescue watch. The Club makes no promises about my safety and no
representations about the condition of its boats, docks, hoists, moorings, grounds,
or any other equipment or facility.

### My assumption of risk

I choose to take part anyway. **I voluntarily assume all risks of these activities,
known and unknown, including the risk that the Club itself may be negligent.**

### My release of the Club, including for its negligence

**I release the Club from all claims for injury, death, or damage to me or my
property arising from these activities or my presence on club property—including
claims that the Club was negligent.** This release is not limited to the risks
inherent in sailing: it also covers the Club's own ordinary negligence, such as
negligence in instruction, supervision, rescue, or the selection, maintenance, and
repair of boats, equipment, and facilities. It binds me, my heirs, my dependents,
and my personal representatives.

### What this release does not cover

This release does not apply to gross negligence, recklessness, or intentional
misconduct, and it does not waive any right that Alaska law does not allow me to
waive.

### My promise not to sue, and to indemnify

I will not sue or bring any claim against the Club for anything I have released
above. If my conduct, or the conduct of a guest I bring, causes injury, death, or
damage and the Club faces a claim or expense because of it, I will indemnify the
Club: I will pay those costs, including reasonable attorney's fees.

### Medical treatment

If I am injured or become ill during club activities, I consent to emergency medical
care, including evacuation and rescue. I am solely responsible for all costs of that
care, evacuation, and rescue.

### The club's rules

I agree to follow the Club's published rules and safety policies, including its life
jacket policy, at all times on club property and during club activities.

## Part Two—Parents and guardians signing for a minor

I sign this Part separately for each minor child. "My child" means the minor named
in my signature record for this Part.

### My own claims

I release the Club from all claims **of my own**—including claims for my child's
medical costs and my own losses—arising from my child's participation in club
activities, including claims that the Club was negligent. The release, carve-outs,
indemnity, and medical-consent terms of Part One apply to this release the same way
they apply to my own participation.

### Release of my child's claims under Alaska law

Alaska Statute 09.65.292 allows a parent to release a child's future negligence
claims against a provider of sports or recreational activities. **I now make that
election: I release my child's future claims against the Club for negligence arising
from the activities named in this document, including sailing, sailing instruction,
capsize and recovery drills, cold-water immersion, club-boat use, racing, and
presence on club grounds and docks.**

**I understand that I cannot waive, and this document does not waive, my child's
claims for reckless or intentional misconduct.**

### Who may sign

I attest that I am, for this child (Alaska Statute 09.65.292(c)):

- a natural or adoptive parent; or
- a legal guardian or court-appointed representative; or
- a representative of an agency with custody of the child; or
- the holder of a power of attorney for the child; or
- a grandparent, aunt, uncle, or adult sibling who resides with the child.

I also consent to emergency medical treatment for my child on the terms of Part One,
and I agree that my child will follow the Club's rules and life jacket policy.

## Signing

I agree that typing my legal name and clicking Sign creates a binding electronic
signature with the same force as a handwritten one, and that this agreement is
governed by Alaska law.

**I HAVE READ THIS ENTIRE AGREEMENT AND I UNDERSTAND IT. I KNOW I AM GIVING UP LEGAL
RIGHTS, INCLUDING THE RIGHT TO SUE THE CLUB FOR ITS NEGLIGENCE. THIS IS A BINDING
LEGAL AGREEMENT, AND I SIGN IT FREELY.**

## Drafting notes for the attorney (not part of the signed document)

1. **Starting point.** Drafted from the club's current Release of Liability
   Agreement (MembershipWorks join form, captured 2026-07-18). That document never
   uses the word "negligence," names no specific risks, and has no minors
   provision—the defects this draft cures. Its medical-consent,
   indemnification, and free-will elements are carried forward.
2. **Donahue six factors** (Donahue v. Ledgends, 331 P.3d 342 (Alaska 2014)): risks
   specifically named (cold-water immersion first, per Alaska's specific-risks
   doctrine); "negligence" used expressly in the assumption, release, and minors
   sections; short sentences, bold emphasis, plain structure; carve-out avoids
   public-policy overreach; the beyond-inherent-risks scope is stated expressly
   ("not limited to the risks inherent in sailing"); the no-supervision section
   makes an affirmative anti-representation statement instead of any safety claim.
3. **Class coverage** (ratified decision 9): instruction risks are named expressly
   (on-water training, capsize/recovery drills, cold water, club boats) so this one
   annual document covers class participation with no separate class waiver. Please
   confirm the single-document coverage.
4. **Racing.** Casual club racing is named among the activities. Race ENTRY
   documents for sanctioned regattas remain separate (RRS Rule 82 bars
   indemnification language there); that document rides the events redesign.
5. **Minors.** Part Two separates the parent's own release from the AS 09.65.292
   election, states the reckless/intentional non-waiver from subsection (b), and
   enumerates the (c) signer categories as an attestation. Open questions: does
   "in writing, signed by the parent" accept this electronic form; how should the
   club treat the qualifying-relative category as a matter of policy?
6. **Fellow participants.** The current document releases "fellow participants";
   this draft folds members into the defined "Club" term instead. Confirm that
   scope is wanted.
7. **Guests.** Guest coverage here is limited to the member's indemnity for guest
   conduct. A signed guest waiver remains a separate (post-v1) document; the
   member-expectations rule requiring hosts to collect signed guest liability forms
   continues to apply meanwhile.
