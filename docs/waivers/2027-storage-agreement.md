# Alaska Sailing Club—Dry Storage Agreement

**Season: 2027.** This agreement is between Alaska Sailing Club, Inc. ("the Club")
and the member signing below ("I," "me"). It covers the seasonal storage space and
the property I identify in my signature record—an RV space on Trailer Row, a
trailered boat space, or a rack space—and I sign it each season with my storage
fee. In the release and indemnity sections, "the Club" includes its officers,
directors, volunteers, and members.

## 1. The assignment

The Club licenses me one storage space for the 2027 season. The space is personal
to me: it is not transferable, it does not pass to a new owner if I sell what is
stored there, and it requires that I hold Active Participating Member status, keep
my fees current, and follow the rules for my storage kind.

## 2. Self-service storage—no custody, no bailment

This is self-service storage. I bring, keep, and remove my own property; I keep my
own keys; the Club never takes possession, custody, or control of anything I store.
This agreement is a license to use space, not a promise to keep, watch, or care for
my property, and it creates no bailment.

## 3. The lot is not secure

**The storage areas are unfenced, unattended, and unmonitored.** There is no gate,
no camera, no lighting for security, and no one watching. The Club makes no
representation of any kind about security. Everything I store stays at my own risk.

## 4. The risks I accept

I accept these risks to my stored property:

- **Theft and vandalism** on an open, unsecured lot.
- **Weather**: wind, storms, snow load, and falling trees and limbs.
- **Fire—including fire spreading from another member's property.** Boats, RVs,
  propane, and batteries stored near each other raise this risk.
- **Wildlife**, and the ordinary hazards of an undeveloped, wooded site.
- **Club lot operations**: vehicles, trailers, and equipment moving on the site.

## 5. My release of the Club, including for its negligence

**I release the Club from all claims for damage to, loss of, or theft of my stored
property—including claims that the Club was negligent.** This release is not
limited to the risks listed above: it also covers the Club's own ordinary
negligence in laying out, maintaining, and operating the storage areas. It binds
me, my heirs, and my personal representatives, and I will not sue the Club over
anything released here.

This release does not apply to gross negligence, recklessness, or intentional
misconduct, and it does not waive any right that Alaska law does not allow me to
waive.

## 6. My indemnification of the Club

**I am responsible for harm my stored property causes.** If my RV, boat,
trailer, or gear—or anything that leaks, falls, rolls, or spreads from it,
including fire and fuel—damages other property, injures any person, or triggers a
remediation or other obligation the Club owes the Matanuska-Susitna Borough, and a
claim or cost of any kind lands on the Club because of it, I will defend and
indemnify the Club and pay those costs, including reasonable attorney's fees.

## 7. Borough land—the obligations that come with it

Trailer Row, the tenting area, and the trailered boat parking sit on land owned by
the Matanuska-Susitna Borough. The Club manages that land under Management
Agreement MSB006789 and the Borough's land use permit conditions, and my use of a
storage space is subject to both. In particular:

- **The 72-hour move-out.** The Borough can require stored RVs and property moved
  within 72 hours of notice. **I agree that if the Club notifies me, I will move my
  property within 72 hours, and I affirm that I am prepared to do that on notice
  and that my property will remain movable at all times.** If I do not move it, the
  Club may tow or move it at my expense.
- **Reachability.** A 72-hour clock only works if the Club can reach me. I will
  keep my contact information current, and I agree that notice sent to the email
  address and phone number on file is notice to me, with the clock running from
  when it is sent.
- **No fuel storage.** Petroleum storage is prohibited on the property. I will not
  store gasoline, kerosene, or diesel in or around my space (propane in
  fit-for-purpose canisters is allowed), and I will report any fuel or chemical
  release to the board immediately. Remediation of a spill from my property is my
  cost, per Section 6.
- **Condition.** I will keep my property and space in the condition my storage
  kind's rules require—for an RV, livable and sanitary inside and out—and the
  Borough may inspect the property it owns at any time.

## 8. Insurance—board decision, presented both ways

> **OPTION A—insurance required.** I will carry insurance on my stored property,
> including liability coverage, for the full season, and I will provide the Club a
> current certificate of insurance [naming Alaska Sailing Club, Inc. as an
> additional insured / on request] before the season starts. My coverage lapsing
> ends my storage assignment.

> **OPTION B—insurance strongly recommended, not required.** Insurance on my
> stored property is my responsibility. The Club's insurance does not cover my
> property, and the Club strongly recommends I carry my own; if my uninsured
> property causes harm, Section 6 still applies in full.

## 9. Club moves

The Club may move my trailer, boat, or other stored property when reasonably
necessary for club activities, safety, site work, or Borough compliance, and will
notify me when it does. I consent to those moves. A move under this section does
not create custody of my property or any ongoing duty of care, and this
agreement's release applies to it.

## 10. Lien, abandonment, and disposition

**Unpaid fees and abandoned property are the one storage problem this agreement
must solve in advance, so these terms are explicit.**

- **Lien.** I grant the Club a lien on my stored property for unpaid storage fees,
  towing and moving costs under Sections 7 and 9, and the Club's costs of removal,
  storage, and sale under this section.
- **When property is deemed abandoned.** My stored property is deemed abandoned if
  any of these happens and the property is still on site [30] days after the Club
  sends notice: my storage fees lapse unpaid; my membership ends; my storage
  assignment ends and the removal deadline passes; or my property is inoperable or
  derelict and I have not responded to the Club.
- **Notice.** Notice under this section goes to my contact information on file and
  by certified mail to my last known mailing address, and states what the Club
  intends to do and the deadline for me to act.
- **Disposition.** After the notice period runs, the Club may remove and store the
  property at my expense, and may sell it at public sale. Sale proceeds apply
  first to what I owe (fees, towing, storage, and costs of sale); any surplus is
  returned to me at my last known address. I remain responsible for any shortfall.

## Signing

I agree that typing my legal name and clicking Sign creates a binding electronic
signature with the same force as a handwritten one, and that this agreement is
governed by Alaska law.

**I HAVE READ THIS ENTIRE AGREEMENT AND I UNDERSTAND IT. I KNOW THE LOT IS NOT
SECURE, I AM STORING MY PROPERTY AT MY OWN RISK, AND I AM GIVING UP LEGAL RIGHTS,
INCLUDING THE RIGHT TO SUE THE CLUB FOR ITS NEGLIGENCE. THIS IS A BINDING LEGAL
AGREEMENT, AND I SIGN IT FREELY.**

## Drafting notes for the attorney (not part of the signed document)

1. **Shape.** A standalone bilateral storage agreement, separate from the general
   release, per the stored-property research: storage duties are bailment and
   contract law, every surveyed facility uses a standalone agreement, and Alaska's
   statutory gaps require contractual lien and abandonment authority no release
   supplies. One agreement covers all three dry storage kinds; the kind-specific
   rules are separate signed acknowledgements.
2. **The clause checklist** from the research maps: no-bailment (§2), named risks
   with "negligence" explicit (§§4–5), truthful unsecured statement (§3),
   exculpation scoped to ordinary negligence with the carve-out (§5, avoiding the
   Sunny Isles internal-contradiction trap), member indemnification (§6),
   insurance both ways (§8), lien/abandonment machinery (§10), club-operations
   moves without implied custody (§9), annual re-sign tied to the fee (header, §1).
3. **Section 10 is the highest-risk section and needs the closest review.** Alaska
   has no self-storage lien statute (HB 97 died in the Senate in 2024—please
   re-verify current status). The contractual machinery must be checked against:
   AS 34.35.220/.225 (general storage lien: three-month clock, public auction,
   statutory penalty of market value +50% for unauthorized sale), AS 28.11
   (abandoned vehicles—covers RVs and boat trailers as registered vehicles), and
   AS 30.30.100–.140 (vessel disposal; its article may be limited to repair
   businesses—scope question). Selling without squarely applicable authority is
   strict-liability conversion; good faith is no defense. The [30]-day notice
   period is a placeholder (industry practice runs 14–90 days).
4. **The 72-hour covenant (§7)** flows down the Borough instruments. Verified
   2026-07-18: the management agreement's text contains no 72-hour or RV language;
   the club's published RV rules attribute it to "the Mat-Su Borough Land Use
   Permit"—the pre-2022 permit (in place since 2013 per the Borough manager's
   Apr–Jun 2022 quarterly report). **The club does not hold a posted copy of that
   permit; see the board packet's discrepancy memo** for the records-request path.
   Until the permit text is verified, the covenant tracks the club's published
   rules, which are the club's own binding statement of the obligation.
5. **Indemnification chain.** §6's Borough-obligation language mirrors MSB006789
   §§19–21 (spill remediation at the club's sole expense; club responsibility for
   safety of all persons; club indemnifies the Borough for members' acts). The
   member-to-club indemnity is what keeps that exposure from resting on the club
   alone. Flag: AS 45.45.900 voids sole-negligence property indemnification in
   construction contracts—confirm no analogous skepticism reaches this context.
6. **Property-damage exculpation in Alaska** has no case squarely on point; the
   Donahue line applies by analogy (the research's Q3). The draft meets the
   stricter personal-injury standard anyway.
7. **Trailer Row Use Guidelines.** The RV eligibility rules already require a
   signed guidelines copy each season; the RV acknowledgement document absorbs that
   existing paper practice—this agreement adds the contract substance on top.
