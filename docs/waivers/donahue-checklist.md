# The pre-publish checklist for signable documents

> The content gate for every release, acknowledgement, and agreement version this
> club publishes, from the waivers design spec (2026-07-17). A new document version
> passes every applicable item before its status moves to `published`. Releases and
> agreements take the whole list; acknowledgements take items 7–10. The checklist
> encodes Alaska's controlling six-factor test (Donahue v. Ledgends, 331 P.3d 342
> (Alaska 2014)) plus the club's own standing rules.

## For releases and agreements (exculpatory documents)

1. **Risks specifically and clearly named.** The document lists the actual risks of
   the actual activity—for anything on or near the water, cold-water immersion
   (cold shock, hypothermia) is named explicitly. No boilerplate-only clause
   ("water sports are hazardous") stands alone.
2. **The word "negligence" appears** in every clause that waives or releases
   negligence claims. A release that never says "negligence" does not release
   negligence claims in Alaska.
3. **Clear, emphasized, simple.** Short sentences, plain words, bold or capitals on
   the operative give-up-rights language, and a final emphasized
   I-understand-what-I-signed block.
4. **No public-policy overreach.** The reckless/intentional-misconduct carve-out is
   present, and nothing purports to waive what Alaska law says cannot be waived.
5. **Exculpation beyond inherent risks is stated expressly.** If the document
   covers the club's own ordinary negligence (ours all do), it says so in those
   words; it never relies on broad general phrases to reach negligence silently.
6. **No safety or maintenance representations.** The document never describes an
   inspection regime, a maintenance practice, a security measure, or any safety
   standard. Statements of what the club does NOT do (no supervision, no
   monitoring, unsecured lot) are required; statements of what it does are
   forbidden here—describing care invites the court to measure the club against
   it.

## For every signable document

7. **A new version, never an edit.** A published document's text is frozen; changes
   ship as a new season version (the freeze guard enforces the hash).
8. **Minors language intact where applicable.** A document minors sign under keeps
   the parent's own release separate from the AS 09.65.292 election, and the
   election names the released activities and the reckless/intentional non-waiver.
9. **Consistent with its siblings.** Risk allocation lives in the release and the
   agreements; acknowledgements carry rules only. No document contradicts another
   (an internally inconsistent packet is how exculpatory clauses get voided
   entirely).
10. **Attorney sign-off recorded** for any substantive change to legal effect—new
    clauses, changed scope, changed carve-outs. Wording-only edits (typos,
    formatting) need the freeze-guard version bump but not fresh counsel review.
