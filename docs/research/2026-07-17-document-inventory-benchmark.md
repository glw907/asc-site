# Research: peer-club waiver and consent document inventory (2026-07-17)

> Commissioned for docs/2026-07-17-member-waivers-design.md (Appendix C distills it
> into the board-facing table; this file is the full report with sources, preserved
> for the drafting sitting and the board packet). Entries are marked [confirmed] (a
> real, fetched document) or [general guidance] (best-practice commentary); a
> guidance mark is not evidence the item is unnecessary.

## (a) The inventory with sources

1. **General liability release / assumption of risk.** Every adult; parent for
   minors; annual or one-time varies by club. [confirmed] US Sailing "Non-Regatta
   Waiver and Release of Liability" template (built-in u18 parent line); Sail Sand
   Point participant agreement (signed once); Cal Sailing Club standing waiver;
   Community Boating Providence combined waiver.
2. **Regatta/event entry waiver, separate by rule.** RRS Rule 82 PROHIBITS
   indemnification language in a race entry document, so the general waiver text
   cannot serve race entries under US Sailing sanction; US Sailing's regatta
   template is release-only, unlike its non-regatta version. Rule 81 commentary: a
   Notice-of-Race waiver clause alone is often insufficient without a signed entry
   waiver. [confirmed, ussailing.org Rule 81/82 prescriptions]
3. **Code of conduct / rules acknowledgement.** Annual with renewal. [confirmed]
   Kirkland Sailing Club (four distinct instruments); Ogden Pineview Yacht Club
   (good standing requires dues AND a separately signed rules doc); US Sailing/Lake
   Bluff YC code of conduct with SafeSport-referencing youth/adult distinctions.
4. **Medical & emergency information / consent-to-treat.** Parent for minors;
   per-season (info changes). [confirmed] University Yacht Club two-part packet
   (info form + separate consent form with treatment authorization and cost
   language); Quassapaug Sailing Center standalone camper emergency form
   (denial-of-participation clause if unsigned); Community Sailing Center Burlington
   (allergy form countersigned by a licensed medical professional); US Sailing
   medical form.
5. **Photo/media release, adults.** Standing, sometimes annual re-opt. [confirmed]
   Community Boating Providence (embedded, irrevocable grant); US Sailing/Lake Bluff
   standalone "Depiction Release".
6. **Photo/media release, minors.** Best peer practice is a separate, revocable,
   item-level opt-in (Quassapaug), vs. UYC's fold-into-release; a standalone form
   also exists (Warriors Soccer Club). Given the club's public site publishes youth
   photos, the Quassapaug model is closest to best practice.
7. **Minor participant waiver + parental consent.** Same cadence as (1). [confirmed]
   US Sailing template's u18 block; UYC parent-signed release.
8. **Volunteer waiver (work parties, tools).** [general guidance] Optimist
   International adult volunteer release template; nonprofit risk guidance (LawHelp
   DC). NO sailing club was found publishing a dedicated tool-use waiver — a real
   sector gap, not evidence of low need; pragmatic path is naming work parties and
   tool use in the general release's risks.
9. **Guest/visitor waiver.** Per visit or standing. [confirmed] Cal Sailing Club
   standing waiver; Riverside Boat Club waiver required before entering the
   facility.
10. **Club-boat use / charter agreement (qualification + damage terms).**
    [confirmed] Island Yacht Club Small Boat Program waiver (equipment checkout
    condition bundled with liability waiver and photo release); Willamette Sailing
    Club boat-usage waiver [unconfirmed, link 404]; damage-deposit structure
    otherwise from commercial charter norms [general guidance].
11. **Mooring agreement.** Annual. [confirmed] Chester Yacht Club mooring policy &
    agreement (sole-risk release, insurance >= CAD $1M, reissued annually); St.
    Thomas Yacht Club (hold-harmless); Rockland YC & Morro Bay YC ($250k/$500k
    minimums, club as additional insured); Deer Isle Yacht Club — the closest real
    analog to unlabeled swing moorings: places tackle condition/adequacy on the
    member and names the risk of a dragging mooring endangering those who assist.
12. **Dry storage agreement.** Annual. [confirmed] Severn Sailing Association boat
    storage application (annual, due Dec 1; explicit release for damage/loss/theft
    while stored; explicit "NOT a secure harbor during severe weather" disclaimer —
    directly on point for an unsecured lot); Santa Rosa Yacht and Boat Club (bundles
    mooring and dry-storage disclaimers into operating rules instead).
13. **Abandoned-property / lien clause within the storage agreement.** [confirmed]
    SSA clause authorizing removal "at my risk and expense" on fee lapse, disrepair,
    or membership lapse. Statutory backing exists elsewhere (Michigan Act 362 of
    1998, naming yacht clubs; Maine Title 10 ch. 212-A); NO Alaska equivalent found
    — attorney must confirm, since without statute the clause rests on contract
    alone.
14. **Proof of insurance for moored/stored boats.** Annual. [confirmed at Chester/
    Rockland/Morro Bay; general guidance for amounts] Port of Bellingham >= $300k;
    Chubb and industry commentary. Unevenly adopted among small clubs — SSA's
    otherwise thorough agreement shows no insurance mandate, the exact gap small
    volunteer clubs leave.
15. **SafeSport / youth-protection acknowledgement.** Instructors and youth-contact
    volunteers; full course every 4 years, annual refresher. [confirmed] US Sailing
    SafeSport handbook and MAAPP policy per the US Center for SafeSport.
16. **Cold-water immersion risk language (inside releases, not a separate form).**
    NO real club waiver was found naming cold shock/hypothermia explicitly — a
    genuine peer-practice gap. Risk science: Alaska DNR cold-water paddling
    guidance; NWS Anchorage/Kodiak paddle-craft risk; RYA cold-water shock guidance.
    Alaska law (Donahue; Langlois; Crowson Law commentary) requires specific risk
    disclosure over boilerplate, making a generic "risk of drowning" clause a real
    enforceability weakness in Alaska. The single clearest Alaska-specific drafting
    improvement available.
17. **The club's own existing baseline.** The legacy site already requires a Release
    of Liability Agreement and the Rules of the Alaska Sailing Club at signup —
    pull from ~/Projects/aksailingclub-legacy and evaluate against this inventory;
    never draft from a blank page.
18. **Insurer risk-management templates.** US Sailing's endorsed Gowrie/Burgee
    program (Chubb-underwritten) supplies emergency action plan, junior safety
    guide, club safety manual templates. [general guidance] Could not verify whether
    coverage is conditioned on specific signed waivers — ask the club's insurer
    directly.

## (b) Bundling patterns

Peer practice splits between heavily bundled (Community Boating Providence: one
umbrella with three signature sections; Island YC: boat-use + photo in one; Santa
Rosa: mooring + storage disclaimers in operating rules) and deliberately separated
(Kirkland: four instruments; the US Sailing/Lake Bluff championship packet: five
documents each with its own signature; UYC juniors: five parent-signed documents).
Consistent structural rule across all: race entry waivers are ALWAYS separate (RRS
82 compliance, not style). Photo consent is the most inconsistently handled item;
the separate revocable opt-in is closer to best practice for a club publishing
youth photos. For a club with this activity mix, a single master waiver is not what
comparable full-service clubs do; the realistic model is a small family of
documents.

## (c) Youth-program specifics

Medical form contents (UYC + Quassapaug + US Sailing models): multiple emergency
contacts with pickup-authorization flagged separately; chronic-condition checklist
(asthma, diabetes, cardiac, epilepsy, food/medication/insect allergies); current
medications; physician and insurance carrier/policy; explicit authorization for
named club agents to consent to emergency treatment plus parental cost agreement.
Burlington's stricter variant requires medical-professional countersignature on the
allergy section. Media consent for minors: range runs from folded-in (UYC) to fully
separate revocable per-item consent (Quassapaug) to a standalone form (Warriors).
COPPA governs online data COLLECTION from under-13s by child-directed services; a
published photo is COPPA-adjacent, not squarely regulated; verifiable parental
consent before publishing remains best practice [general guidance — flag for the
attorney, not settled law].

## (d) Commonly missed, flagged for the attorney

1. Alaska storage-lien statute unconfirmed (none found); the abandonment clause may
   rest on contract alone.
2. Cold-water immersion named in no real waiver found, despite Alaska's
   specific-risks doctrine.
3. Insurance-proof requirements unevenly adopted among small clubs (SSA's gap).
4. No sailing club publishes a volunteer tool-use waiver; sector-wide gap.
5. Race waivers must be separate documents under RRS 82.
6. The club's existing baseline documents are the starting point.
7. Mooring-tackle condition language matters specifically for unlabeled swing
   moorings (Deer Isle's model: member responsibility, dragging-mooring risk to
   those who assist).
